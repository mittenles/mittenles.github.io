Southwest to Hipmunk – Landing on two different travel sites: a comparison of Space, Size, Balance

How do these two websites use Space as a design element?

Hipmunk and Southwest both use a significant amount of negative space, though the treatment of the space does differ.
The negative space in Southwest's homepage includes an image, one that is seasonally appropriate and ostensibly will change to reflect the primary motivating reasons people visit the site.

How do these sites use Size as a design element?

In the case of Southwest, the Size of the call to action (book a ticket home for $71 just in time for the holidays) is so large as to draw the eye directly to the element, even if the user was looking for the more universal call to action- the booking elements.

In the case of Hipmunk, the size of the primary two calls to action make up a significant chunk of the prime focal space. Overall, this panel represents at least one fifth of the page.
